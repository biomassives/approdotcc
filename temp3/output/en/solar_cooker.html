Cook With the Sun
Solar Cooker Basics

Solar cooking uses reflective or insulating designs to trap sunlight as heat. It reduces fuel costs, smoke inhalation, and deforestation.

  • Panel Cooker – Folds reflective panels around a dark pot—great for simmering.
  • Box Cooker – Insulated box with glazing; reaches higher temperatures for baking.
  • Parabolic Dish – Concentrates strong heat for frying or boiling quickly.

Use a dark, thin-walled pot with a clear lid. Reposition every 30–45 minutes to follow the sun.

Advanced Builds: Add insulation (wool, straw) under the box, or a secondary glazing layer to boost temperature.

Solar cookers have been tested since the 18th century; modern materials make them far more efficient.

Community Impact: Reduces fuelwood demand and indoor air pollution.

Solar lunch programs, disaster relief kitchens, and refugee camps use cookers to cut fuel costs.

⚙️ Local Innovation

Repurpose reflective rice sacks or metalized snack wrappers as panel surfaces.
